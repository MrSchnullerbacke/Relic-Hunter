ENEMY DESIGN GUIDE

Purpose Enemies should create different combat pressures and tactical
situations rather than only having different health values.

Enemy Design Principles

1.  Clear Role Each enemy must have a defined combat role:

-   Grunt (basic melee)
-   Ranged attacker
-   Tank
-   Swarm unit
-   Support/healer
-   Summoner
-   Area denial enemy

2.  Readable Behavior Players must be able to understand enemy attacks
    quickly through animation, movement, or telegraph indicators.

3.  Combat Pressure Enemies should create different types of pressure:

-   movement pressure
-   positioning pressure
-   crowd pressure
-   time pressure

4.  Synergy Between Enemies Enemy groups should be designed to
    complement each other.

Example combinations: Tank + Ranged Swarm + Area caster Support + Elite

5.  Elite Variants Elite enemies should modify normal enemy behavior
    with special traits such as:

-   fire aura
-   poison trails
-   faster movement
-   shield ability
